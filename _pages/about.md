I am a PhD Candidate in Mathematics and Artificial Intelligence at [Institut Polytechnique de Paris (IP Paris)](https://www.ip-paris.fr/en) within the [SAMOVAR](https://samovar.telecom-sudparis.eu/index.php/en/accueil-2/) laboratory. I am under the supervision of [Joaquin Garcia-Alfaro](https://scn.telecom-sudparis.eu/joaquin/) in the [SCN](https://scn.telecom-sudparis.eu/index.html) team.

My work focuses on developing efficient, safe, robust, and trustworthy Reinforcement Learning (RL) methods for critical cyber-physical systems, with particular emphasis on the physical layer of 6G networks.

My thesis is part of the [PEPR Future Networks](https://pepr-futurenetworks.fr/en/the-pepr/) program, a French strategic and large-scale research initiative under [France 2030](https://www.info.gouv.fr/grand-dossier/france-2030-en), dedicated to advancing next-generation communication technologies.

## 🔬 Research Interests  

My research lies at the intersection of Reinforcement Learning (RL), Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL), and Physical AI. I am particularly interested in the safety, robustness, and scalability of learning-based methods deployed in critical complex physical environments.

A central goal of my work is to bridge the gap between theoretical foundations and real-world applications. I enjoy addressing fundamental research questions while accounting for the constraints, uncertainty, and imperfections encountered outside controlled or idealized settings.

I am especially motivated by projects that span the full research process, from mathematical analysis and algorithm design to experimental validation on real-world prototypes.

I am also interested in the potential of quantum computing to improve the scalability and computational efficiency of Reinforcement Learning.

## 🎓 Academic Background  

I hold a Master’s degree in [Mathematics and Artificial Intelligence](https://www.imo.universite-paris-saclay.fr/fr/etudiants/masters/mathematiques-et-applications/m2/m2-mathematique-et-intelligence-artificielle/) from Paris-Saclay University, where I built strong foundations in statistics, probability, optimization, machine learning, and deep learning.  

Before that, I completed a double Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics and Fundamental Physics at Paris-Saclay University, known for its rigorous and demanding scientific curriculum.  
This background in physics continues to shape my approach to research and fuels my curiosity for topics such as quantum computing and controlled nuclear fusion for civilian applications.
